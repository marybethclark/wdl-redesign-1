EMJ Embedded Systems Blasts Off Aboard the Space Shuttle Discovery With SPACEHAB

In early June, EMJ Embedded Systems got to see the exciting end-result of a long sales cycle. When the Space Shuttle Discovery was launched on June 2, products that we sold went up with it and were used to establish a continuous, reliable communication channel between earth and the shuttle. In the future those same products will be used to allow communications between earth and orbiting space stations.

SPACEHAB’s Universal Communications System (SHUCS) made its first flight aboard Discovery. It is comprised of an internal terminal, which operates much like a cellular phone, and an external piece that serves as an antenna to communicate with a satellite. By using the commercial Inmarsat satellite and ground station communication networks operation on the radio frequency spectrum, SHUCS enables earth-based users to interface with an orbiting payload or astronaut via the Internet and COMSAT Mobile Communications using the Inmarsat system. SHUCS can accommodate file transfers, up/downlink fax and voice communication.

To make all this possible, SPACEHAB contracted with CrossLink, Inc. of Boulder, Colorado to identify and integrate the mission’s hardware and software products for cost-efficient, high-speed data gathering, processing, and transmission. As systems integrators for SHUCS, CrossLink contacted EMJ looking for a single board computer in PC/104 form factor. The EMJ salesperson listened to CrossLink, thought about the application and recommended several other products. Ultimately, EMJ provided Crosslink with a JUMPtec PC/104 586 133MHz single board computer with Ethernet onboard, a parvus Versinet Ethernet board, a parvus PC/104 enclosure and fan card, and M-Systems IDE Flash.

Because EMJ Embedded Systems was able to supply the needed information and products, CrossLink could spend their time on development instead of procurement. SPACEHAB went up with the communications system they needed. And EMJ had the opportunity to be involved in an exciting and cutting-edge project.
